Felipe Jun Iglesia

About Felipe

Malaysian pianist, arranger, and composer for film and video games Felipe Jun Iglesia revels in his passion for music. He has been in many roles, including being the President, Producer, Arranger, and Music Director for the International College of Music's Video Game Tribute (VGT) Concert in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The concert brought in around 400 audience members with music that was prepared and conducted primarily by Felipe himself. He now studies in Berklee College of Music under Film and Games Scoring to hone his craft passion in scoring for the visual arts. He has scored for a number of games, ranging from personal collaborative projects to ongoing development teams in UCLA and is now setting foot in to the Hollywood industry. Felipe continues to improve in his ability as a truly well-rounded musicians and find opportunities to lead and collaborate. His ongoing projects include scoring for visuals and continuing his legacy with VGT in the United States.

Facts

Inspirations

His biggest inspirations when writing for media include, Evan Call (Violet Evergarden), Max LL (Spiritfarer), Lena Raine (Celeste, Minecraft), and many more.

Performances

He has performed at many concerts including the Malaysian Jazz Piano Festival and represented the International College of Music (ICOM) in multiple other Malaysian school festivals.

Origins

He has been pursuing music professionally since he was 16. He switched over from guitar to piano as his principal instrument and has been pursuing contemporary styles such as jazz and fusion.

Favorite Games

Omori, Ace Attorney, Breath of the Wild, Spiritfarer

Spark of Passion

The spark for his passion for music was the anime Violet Evergarden due to the incredibly emotional and beautiful soundtrack by Evan Call.

Favorite Artists

His favorite music artists include Porter Robinson, Casiopea, Hiromi, Penthouse, and Insane In The Rain.
